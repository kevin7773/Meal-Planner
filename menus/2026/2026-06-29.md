## Monday, June 29 - Blackstone Chicken Fajita Soft Tacos

**Recipe:** FDP-0005 rev 1 (candidate)

## Recipe Card

- **Active prep:** 20 minutes
- **Cook time:** 15 minutes
- **Cooking method:** Blackstone
- **Estimated fiber:** 12 grams per serving
- **Kid-friendly design:** Familiar soft tacos with mild seasoning and optional vegetables
- **Best seasons:** Spring and summer
- **Schedule fit:** Mexican Monday

## Ingredients

### Main Ingredients

- 1 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts, thinly sliced
- 3 bell peppers, sliced
- 1 large yellow onion, sliced
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 8 small whole-wheat tortillas
- 1 (15-ounce) can pinto beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 cup frozen corn, thawed

### Seasonings

- 2 teaspoons chili powder
- 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon onion powder
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

### Toppings and Sides

- 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
- 1 cup salsa
- 1/2 cup sour cream
- 1 lime, cut into wedges
- 4 cups watermelon

## Directions

1. Mix the chili powder, ground cumin, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion
   powder, kosher salt, and black pepper.
2. Toss the chicken, bell peppers, and onion with the olive oil and seasoning
   mixture.
3. Cook on a medium-high Blackstone for 10-15 minutes, turning regularly, until
   the chicken reaches 165 F and the vegetables are tender.
4. Warm the tortillas, pinto beans, and corn separately.
5. Let everyone build two tacos with chicken, cheddar, salsa, sour cream, and
   lime. Serve peppers and onions separately when preferred, with pinto beans,
   corn, and watermelon.

## Leftover Plan

- Extra chicken and vegetables can become lunch quesadillas.

## Wednesday, July 01 - Blackstone Hibachi Chicken Fried Rice

**Recipe:** FDP-0009 rev 1 (candidate)

## Recipe Card

- **Active prep:** 20 minutes
- **Cook time:** 40 minutes
- **Cooking method:** Blackstone and stovetop
- **Estimated fiber:** 10 grams per serving
- **Kid-friendly design:** Familiar chicken and fried rice with sauce served on the side
- **Best seasons:** Spring and summer
- **Schedule fit:** Any day except Mexican Monday

## Ingredients

### Main Ingredients

- 1 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts, diced
- 2 cups dry brown rice
- 3 1/2 cups water
- 2 large eggs, beaten
- 2 cups frozen peas and carrots
- 1 zucchini, finely diced
- 1 tablespoon canola oil
- 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
- 2 teaspoons toasted sesame oil
- 1 tablespoon honey

### Seasonings

- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

## Directions

1. Combine the brown rice, water, and 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt. Bring to a
   boil, cover, reduce the heat, and simmer for 35-40 minutes.
2. Whisk the soy sauce, toasted sesame oil, honey, garlic powder, ground ginger,
   remaining 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt, and black pepper. Reserve half as a
   table sauce.
3. Heat the Blackstone to medium-high. Cook the chicken in the canola oil for
   7-9 minutes until it reaches 165 F.
4. Add the peas, carrots, and zucchini and cook for 4 minutes. Move everything
   aside and scramble the eggs until fully set.
5. Add the rice and half the seasoned soy sauce. Toss for 3-4 minutes. Serve
   the remaining sauce separately. This recipe intentionally uses no
   mayonnaise-based hibachi sauce.

## Leftover Plan

- Extra fried rice can be reheated for lunch.

## Thursday, July 02 - Slow-Cooker BBQ Chicken Baked Potato Bar

**Recipe:** FDP-0006 rev 1 (candidate)

## Recipe Card

- **Active prep:** 15 minutes
- **Cook time:** 4-6 hours
- **Cooking method:** Slow cooker
- **Estimated fiber:** 11 grams per serving
- **Kid-friendly design:** Familiar barbecue chicken with customizable potato toppings
- **Best seasons:** Year-round
- **Schedule fit:** Tuesday or Thursday

## Ingredients

### Main Ingredients

- 2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts
- 1 cup barbecue sauce
- 4 large russet potatoes
- 1 (15-ounce) can vegetarian baked beans
- 2 cups broccoli florets

### Seasonings

- 1 1/2 teaspoons smoked paprika
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon onion powder
- 3/4 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

### Toppings and Sides

- 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese

## Directions

1. Put the chicken in the slow cooker. Mix the barbecue sauce, smoked paprika,
   garlic powder, onion powder, kosher salt, and black pepper and pour it over
   the chicken.
2. Cover and cook on low for 5-6 hours or high for 4 hours, until the chicken
   reaches 165 F and shreds easily.
3. During the final 20 minutes, pierce the potatoes and microwave them for
   12-16 minutes, turning halfway, until fork-tender.
4. Steam the broccoli for 5-6 minutes and warm the baked beans.
5. Shred the chicken. Split each potato and let everyone add BBQ chicken,
   cheddar, broccoli, and baked beans as desired.

## Leftover Plan

- Extra BBQ chicken can be used in lunch wraps or quesadillas.

## Saturday, July 04 - Grilled Salmon Rice Bowls with Fruit

**Recipe:** FDP-0011 rev 1 (candidate)

## Recipe Card

- **Active prep:** 15 minutes
- **Cook time:** 40 minutes
- **Cooking method:** Grill and stovetop
- **Estimated fiber:** 9 grams per serving
- **Kid-friendly design:** Established family-favorite salmon with simple rice and fruit
- **Best seasons:** Spring and summer
- **Schedule fit:** Any day except Mexican Monday

## Ingredients

### Main Ingredients

- 1 1/2 pounds salmon, cut into 4 fillets
- 1 1/2 cups dry brown rice
- 2 3/4 cups water
- 1 (15-ounce) can pinto beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 1/2 cups frozen corn, thawed
- 1 tablespoon olive oil

### Seasonings

- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon dried dill
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt, divided
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

### Toppings and Sides

- 1 lemon, cut into wedges
- 2 cups mixed berries
- 2 cups watermelon

## Directions

1. Combine the brown rice, water, and 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt. Bring to a
   boil, cover, reduce the heat, and simmer for 35-40 minutes.
2. Brush the salmon with olive oil. Sprinkle it with garlic powder, dried dill,
   remaining 3/4 teaspoon kosher salt, and black pepper.
3. Grill skin-side down over medium heat for 4-6 minutes per side, until the
   salmon reaches 145 F.
4. Warm the pinto beans and corn separately.
5. Build bowls with brown rice and salmon. Offer pinto beans and corn
   separately, and serve with lemon wedges and fruit.

## Leftover Plan

- Extra salmon and rice can become lunch bowls.

## Sunday, July 05 - Turkey Cheesesteak Bowls

**Recipe:** FDP-0003 rev 1 (candidate)

## Recipe Card

- **Active prep:** 20 minutes
- **Cook time:** 30 minutes
- **Cooking method:** Blackstone and stovetop
- **Estimated fiber:** 10 grams per serving
- **Kid-friendly design:** Cheesy turkey and rice-style bowl with vegetables that can be served separately
- **Best seasons:** Spring and summer
- **Schedule fit:** Any day except Mexican Monday

## Ingredients

### Main Ingredients

- 1 1/2 pounds 93%-lean ground turkey
- 1 cup dry farro, rinsed
- 3 cups water
- 2 bell peppers, sliced
- 1 large yellow onion, sliced
- 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 4 slices provolone cheese

### Seasonings

- 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon onion powder
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt, divided
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

### Toppings and Sides

- 2 cups mixed berries

## Directions

1. Combine the farro, water, and 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt. Simmer uncovered for
   25-30 minutes until tender, then drain.
2. Mix the smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, remaining 3/4 teaspoon
   kosher salt, and black pepper.
3. Heat the Blackstone to medium-high. Cook the turkey in the olive oil with
   half the seasoning mixture until it reaches 165 F, 7-9 minutes.
4. Add the bell peppers, onion, mushrooms, and remaining seasoning mixture.
   Cook for 6-8 minutes until tender, then combine with the turkey.
5. Divide the farro and turkey mixture among four bowls. Top each with one
   provolone slice and cover briefly to melt. Serve with mixed berries.

## Leftover Plan

- Use extra filling in omelets, wraps, or lunch bowls.
