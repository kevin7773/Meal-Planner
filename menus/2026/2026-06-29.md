## Monday, June 29 - Grilled Chicken Caesar Salad

**Recipe:** FDP-0040 rev 1 (candidate)
**Planned Diners:** 4

## Recipe Card

- **Active prep:** 20 minutes
- **Cook time:** 15 minutes
- **Cooking method:** Grill
- **Estimated fiber:** 5 grams per serving
- **Kid-friendly design:** Gray Loves It
- **Best seasons:** Spring, Summer, Fall
- **Schedule fit:** Extreme-heat meal; serve one Kid's Cuisine for the son

## Ingredients

### Main Ingredients

- 1 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts
- 2 large romaine hearts, chopped
- 1 cup whole-grain croutons
- 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
- 1/3 cup olive oil
- 3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
- 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
- 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
- 1 garlic clove, finely minced

### Seasonings

- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 3/4 teaspoon kosher salt, divided
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper, divided

## Directions

1. Heat the grill to medium-high.
2. Season the chicken with the garlic powder, 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, and 1/4 teaspoon black pepper.
3. Grill the chicken for 5 to 7 minutes per side until it reaches 165 F. Rest for 5 minutes, then slice.
4. Whisk the olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, minced garlic, remaining 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt, and remaining 1/4 teaspoon black pepper.
5. Toss the romaine with enough dressing to coat lightly. Add the croutons and Parmesan.
6. Top the salad with sliced grilled chicken. Serve the remaining dressing on the side and prepare one Kid's Cuisine for the son.

## Leftover Plan

- Refrigerate chicken and dressing separately for up to 3 days. Do not store dressed romaine.

- **Single Kid's Cuisine (KQM-012):** $3.00; 2 grams fiber per serving

## Tuesday, June 30 - Ground Turkey Hard Shell Tacos with Pinto Beans and Rice

**Recipe:** FDP-0038 rev 1 (candidate)
**Planned Diners:** 4

## Recipe Card

- **Active prep:** 15 minutes
- **Cook time:** 30 minutes
- **Cooking method:** Stovetop
- **Estimated fiber:** 13 grams per serving
- **Kid-friendly design:** Both children like/love it
- **Best seasons:** Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter
- **Schedule fit:** Mexican Monday; components stay separate for easy customization

## Ingredients

### Main Ingredients

- 1 1/4 pounds ground turkey
- 12 hard corn taco shells
- 1 can (15 ounces) pinto beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 cup dry brown rice
- 1 cup frozen corn
- 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
- 1 cup shredded romaine lettuce
- 1 medium tomato, diced
- 1/2 cup mild salsa
- 1/2 cup sour cream
- 2 1/4 cups water, divided
- 1 tablespoon olive oil

### Seasonings

- 2 teaspoons chili powder
- 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon onion powder
- 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 3/4 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

## Directions

1. Combine the brown rice, 2 cups water, and 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, cover, reduce the heat to low, and cook for 25 minutes.
2. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the ground turkey and cook for 6 to 8 minutes, breaking it into small pieces, until no pink remains and it reaches 165 F.
3. Stir the chili powder, ground cumin, garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, remaining 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, black pepper, and remaining 1/4 cup water into the turkey. Simmer for 3 minutes.
4. Warm the pinto beans and corn in a small saucepan over medium heat for 5 minutes.
5. Warm the taco shells according to the package directions.
6. Serve three shells per person with the turkey, cheddar, lettuce, tomato, salsa, and sour cream arranged separately. Serve the rice, pinto beans, and corn alongside.

## Leftover Plan

- Refrigerate extra turkey, rice, beans, and toppings separately for up to 3 days. Use them for taco bowls at lunch.

## Friday, July 03 - Blackstone Breakfast for Dinner

**Recipe:** FDP-0041 rev 1 (candidate)
**Planned Diners:** 4

## Recipe Card

- **Active prep:** 15 minutes
- **Cook time:** 25 minutes
- **Cooking method:** Blackstone
- **Estimated fiber:** 6 grams per serving
- **Kid-friendly design:** Both children like/love it
- **Best seasons:** Spring, Summer, Fall
- **Schedule fit:** Outdoor-cooked dinner for a hot evening

## Ingredients

### Main Ingredients

- 2 cups whole-grain pancake mix
- 1 1/2 cups milk
- 10 large eggs
- 12 turkey sausage links
- 2 cups seasonal fresh fruit
- 4 tablespoons butter, divided
- 1/2 cup maple syrup

### Seasonings

- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

## Directions

1. Prepare the pancake batter with the pancake mix and milk according to the package consistency.
2. Heat the Blackstone to medium. Cook the turkey sausage for 10 to 12 minutes, turning often, until it reaches 165 F.
3. Melt 2 tablespoons butter on a clean section of the griddle. Cook eight pancakes, turning when bubbles form and the bottoms are golden.
4. Whisk the eggs with the kosher salt and black pepper.
5. Melt the remaining 2 tablespoons butter on the griddle. Scramble the eggs for 3 to 5 minutes until set.
6. Serve two pancakes, scrambled eggs, and three sausage links per person with fruit and maple syrup.

## Leftover Plan

- Refrigerate pancakes and sausage separately for up to 3 days. Reheat for breakfast.

## Sunday, July 05 - Turkey and Cheese Deli Sandwich Night

**Recipe:** FDP-0042 rev 1 (candidate)
**Planned Diners:** 4

## Recipe Card

- **Active prep:** 15 minutes
- **Cook time:** 0 minutes
- **Cooking method:** No-cook
- **Estimated fiber:** 7 grams per serving
- **Kid-friendly design:** Both children like/love it
- **Best seasons:** Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter
- **Schedule fit:** No-cook dinner for extreme heat

## Ingredients

### Main Ingredients

- 8 slices whole-grain sandwich bread
- 12 ounces deli turkey
- 8 slices cheddar or provolone cheese
- 8 ounces potato chips
- 4 cups chopped romaine lettuce
- 1 cucumber, sliced
- 2 medium tomatoes, cut into wedges
- 2 cups baby carrots
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
- 2 tablespoons yellow mustard, served on the side

### Seasonings

- 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

## Directions

1. Arrange the bread, deli turkey, cheese, and yellow mustard so everyone can assemble a sandwich without mayonnaise.
2. Combine the romaine, cucumber, tomatoes, and baby carrots in a serving bowl.
3. Whisk the olive oil, apple cider vinegar, dried oregano, kosher salt, and black pepper.
4. Serve the vinaigrette on the side of the garden salad.
5. Serve each sandwich with potato chips and garden salad.

## Leftover Plan

- Refrigerate deli ingredients and undressed salad separately for lunch the next day.
