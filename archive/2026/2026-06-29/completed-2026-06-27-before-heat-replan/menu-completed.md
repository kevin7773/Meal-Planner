## Monday, June 29 - Ground Turkey Hard Shell Tacos with Pinto Beans and Rice

**Recipe:** FDP-0038 rev 1 (candidate)
**Planned Diners:** 4

## Recipe Card

- **Active prep:** 15 minutes
- **Cook time:** 30 minutes
- **Cooking method:** Stovetop
- **Estimated fiber:** 13 grams per serving
- **Kid-friendly design:** Both children like/love it
- **Best seasons:** Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter
- **Schedule fit:** Mexican Monday; components stay separate for easy customization

## Ingredients

### Main Ingredients

- 1 1/4 pounds ground turkey
- 12 hard corn taco shells
- 1 can (15 ounces) pinto beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 cup dry brown rice
- 1 cup frozen corn
- 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
- 1 cup shredded romaine lettuce
- 1 medium tomato, diced
- 1/2 cup mild salsa
- 1/2 cup sour cream
- 2 1/4 cups water, divided
- 1 tablespoon olive oil

### Seasonings

- 2 teaspoons chili powder
- 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon onion powder
- 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 3/4 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

## Directions

1. Combine the brown rice, 2 cups water, and 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, cover, reduce the heat to low, and cook for 25 minutes.
2. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the ground turkey and cook for 6 to 8 minutes, breaking it into small pieces, until no pink remains and it reaches 165 F.
3. Stir the chili powder, ground cumin, garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, remaining 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, black pepper, and remaining 1/4 cup water into the turkey. Simmer for 3 minutes.
4. Warm the pinto beans and corn in a small saucepan over medium heat for 5 minutes.
5. Warm the taco shells according to the package directions.
6. Serve three shells per person with the turkey, cheddar, lettuce, tomato, salsa, and sour cream arranged separately. Serve the rice, pinto beans, and corn alongside.

## Leftover Plan

- Refrigerate extra turkey, rice, beans, and toppings separately for up to 3 days. Use them for taco bowls at lunch.

## Tuesday, June 30 - Slow-Cooker BBQ Chicken Baked Potato Bar

**Recipe:** FDP-0006 rev 1 (candidate)
**Planned Diners:** 4

## Recipe Card

- **Active prep:** 15 minutes
- **Cook time:** 4-6 hours
- **Cooking method:** Slow cooker
- **Estimated fiber:** 11 grams per serving
- **Kid-friendly design:** Familiar barbecue chicken with customizable potato toppings
- **Best seasons:** Year-round
- **Schedule fit:** Tuesday or Thursday

## Ingredients

### Main Ingredients

- 2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts
- 1 cup barbecue sauce
- 4 large russet potatoes
- 1 (15-ounce) can vegetarian baked beans
- 2 cups broccoli florets

### Seasonings

- 1 1/2 teaspoons smoked paprika
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon onion powder
- 3/4 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

### Toppings and Sides

- 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese

## Directions

1. Put the chicken in the slow cooker. Mix the barbecue sauce, smoked paprika,
   garlic powder, onion powder, kosher salt, and black pepper and pour it over
   the chicken.
2. Cover and cook on low for 5-6 hours or high for 4 hours, until the chicken
   reaches 165 F and shreds easily.
3. During the final 20 minutes, pierce the potatoes and microwave them for
   12-16 minutes, turning halfway, until fork-tender.
4. Steam the broccoli for 5-6 minutes and warm the baked beans.
5. Shred the chicken. Split each potato and let everyone add BBQ chicken,
   cheddar, broccoli, and baked beans as desired.

## Leftover Plan

- Extra BBQ chicken can be used in lunch wraps or quesadillas.

## Wednesday, July 01 - Hot Dogs with Macaroni and Cheese and Baked Beans

**Recipe:** FDP-0039 rev 1 (candidate)
**Planned Diners:** 4

## Recipe Card

- **Active prep:** 5 minutes
- **Cook time:** 20 minutes
- **Cooking method:** Stovetop
- **Estimated fiber:** 8 grams per serving
- **Kid-friendly design:** Both children like/love it
- **Best seasons:** Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter
- **Schedule fit:** Familiar complete meal for a standard weeknight

## Ingredients

### Main Ingredients

- 8 hot dogs
- 8 whole-wheat hot dog buns
- 2 boxes (7.25 ounces each) macaroni and cheese
- 2 cans (15 ounces each) vegetarian baked beans
- 1/2 cup milk, or the amount required by the macaroni packages
- 4 tablespoons butter, or the amount required by the macaroni packages

### Seasonings

- No additional seasoning; prepare each familiar component as packaged

## Directions

1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil and cook the macaroni for the time listed on the package.
2. While the macaroni cooks, warm the baked beans in a saucepan over medium-low heat for 8 to 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
3. Place the hot dogs in a skillet with 1/2 cup water. Cover and simmer over medium heat for 6 minutes, turning once, until heated to 165 F.
4. Drain the macaroni. Stir in the packaged cheese mix, milk, and butter until smooth.
5. Place the hot dogs in the buns and serve two per person with macaroni and cheese and baked beans.

## Leftover Plan

- Refrigerate leftover macaroni, beans, and hot dogs separately for up to 3 days.

## Friday, July 03 - Chicken Enchilada

**Recipe:** FDP-0015 rev 2 (candidate)
**Planned Diners:** 3

## Recipe Card

- **Active prep:** 15 minutes
- **Cook time:** 20 minutes
- **Cooking method:** Oven
- **Estimated fiber:** 8 grams per serving
- **Kid-friendly design:** Both children like/love it
- **Best seasons:** Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter
- **Schedule fit:** Complete meal; suitable for a standard weeknight

## Ingredients

### Main Ingredients

- 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
- 1 cup enchilada sauce
- 8 corn tortillas
- 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
- 1/2 cup sour cream
- 1/4 cup chopped green onions
- 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
### Seasonings

- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

## Directions

1. Preheat the oven to 375 F.
2. In a bowl, mix the shredded chicken with 1/2 cup enchilada sauce, 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, and 1/4 teaspoon black pepper.
3. Warm tortillas in a microwave or on a skillet to make them pliable.
4. Fill each tortilla with the chicken mixture, roll them up, and place seam-side down in a baking dish.
5. Pour the remaining enchilada sauce over the rolled tortillas and sprinkle with cheese.
6. Bake in the preheated oven for 20 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
7. Serve topped with sour cream, green onions, and cilantro.

**Recipe:** FDP-0031 rev 1 (candidate)

- 1 tsp Pepper
- 1 tsp Paprika
- 1 tsp Parsley
- 3 tbsp Oil
- 2 lbs Kielbasa Sausage, cut into ? inch chunks
- 2 Medium Zucchinis, cut into ? inch chunks
- 1 Red Bell Pepper, cut into ? inch chunks

### Seasonings

- Review imported ingredients for seasoning classification

## Directions

1. In a large mixing bowl, combine potatoes, onion, garlic, salt, pepper, oil, parsley, and paprika.
