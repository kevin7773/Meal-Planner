# Family Preferences

## Core Requirements

- Plan for a family of four.
- Keep meals kid-friendly, flavorful, and practical.
- Aim for roughly 10-15 grams of fiber per serving when practical.
- Favor fiber from beans, vegetables, fruit, whole grains, and legumes.
- Monday is always Mexican Monday.

Kid-friendly means more than meeting nutrition targets. Favor familiar formats
such as tacos, quesadillas, sliders, burgers, pizza, sandwiches, rice bowls,
baked potatoes, breakfast-for-dinner, and simple salmon plates. Keep spice mild,
make toppings optional, and serve unfamiliar vegetables or sauces separately.
Every proposed recipe must explain why children are likely to accept it.

## Beans

Prefer, in order:

1. Pinto beans
2. Mexican-style pinto beans
3. Refried pinto beans

Avoid black beans unless they are clearly better for the recipe.

## Proteins

Prefer poultry, then beef, then seafood. Use chicken or turkey for tacos,
bowls, burgers, sliders, and pasta when appropriate. Limit beef-centered meals
to roughly every other week. Salmon is welcome occasionally.

Avoid pork as a centerpiece except for bacon, sausage, ham, ham steaks, or
occasional slow-cooker pulled pork.

## Established Favorites

- Tacos and quesadillas
- Chicken fajitas
- BBQ chicken sandwiches or sliders
- Pizza-style pasta and calzones
- Chicken Alfredo tortellini
- Chili
- Salmon
- Spaghetti bake

Reserve chili, soups, spaghetti bake, and heavy casseroles for cooler weather.

## Scheduling

Tuesday and Thursday must be slow-cooker, minimal-cook, or no-cook meals.
Thursday must be included in the second weekly email, which covers Wednesday
through Friday.

## Seasoning

List exact quantities for every seasoning. Explicitly name each seasoning in
the directions instead of using vague instructions such as "season chicken."

## Sauces and Dressings

Do not use mayonnaise in recipes. For coleslaw, use a light apple-cider vinegar
dressing rather than mayonnaise or Greek yogurt.
