# Turkey Cheesesteak Brown Rice Bowls

## Recipe Card

- **Active prep:** 20 minutes
- **Cook time:** 40 minutes
- **Cooking method:** Blackstone and stovetop
- **Estimated fiber:** 10 grams per serving
- **Kid-friendly design:** Cheesy turkey bowl with peppers served separately when preferred
- **Best seasons:** Spring and summer
- **Schedule fit:** Any day except Mexican Monday

## Ingredients

### Main Ingredients

- 1 1/2 pounds 93%-lean ground turkey
- 1 1/2 cups dry brown rice
- 2 3/4 cups water
- 2 bell peppers, sliced
- 1 large yellow onion, sliced
- 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 pound green beans, trimmed

### Seasonings

- 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon onion powder
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt, divided
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

### Toppings and Sides

- 4 slices provolone cheese
- 2 cups mixed berries

## Directions

1. Combine the brown rice, water, and 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt. Bring to a
   boil, cover, reduce the heat, and simmer for 35-40 minutes.
2. Mix the smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, remaining 3/4 teaspoon
   kosher salt, and black pepper.
3. Cook the turkey in the olive oil on a medium-high Blackstone with half the
   seasoning mixture until it reaches 165 F, 7-9 minutes.
4. Add the bell peppers, onion, mushrooms, and remaining seasoning mixture.
   Cook for 6-8 minutes. Keep some vegetables separate when preferred.
5. Steam the green beans for 5-6 minutes. Divide rice and turkey among bowls,
   top each with provolone, and serve with green beans and berries.

## Leftover Plan

- Extra turkey filling can become lunch sandwiches.
