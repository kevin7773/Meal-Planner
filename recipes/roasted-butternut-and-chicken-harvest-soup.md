# Roasted Butternut and Chicken Harvest Soup

## Ingredients

### Main Ingredients

- 2 lb butternut squash, peeled, seeded, cut into 1-inch cubes
- 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 1/2 tsp freshly ground black pepper
- 2 cups small dry pasta (ditalini or small shells)
- 1 lb chicken Italian sweet sausage, casings removed
- 2 tbsp butter
- 2 cups diced leeks, white parts only, rinsed well
- 1 cup diced carrots
- 1/2 cup diced celery (about 1 large stalk)
- 1 tbsp minced garlic
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1/4 tsp crushed red pepper flakes (optional)
- 1 tsp dried sage (or poultry seasoning)
- 6 cups hot chicken stock
- 3 cups baby spinach, roughly chopped
- 1/2 cup finely grated parmesan, plus more for serving
- 1 cup heavy cream
- Kosher salt, to taste

### Seasonings

- Review imported ingredients for seasoning classification

## Directions

1. **Roast the squash**: Preheat oven to 450?F (230?C). Line a sheet pan with foil. Toss squash with 1 tbsp olive oil and black pepper. Roast for 10 minutes, flip, and roast for another 10 minutes until tender and caramelized at the edges. Set aside.
2. **Cook the pasta**: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook pasta to package instructions until al dente. Drain and rinse briefly with cold water. Set aside.
3. **Brown the sausage**: Film the bottom of a medium soup pot with 1 tbsp olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken sausage, break it up, and cook until browned, about 5 minutes.
4. **Build the flavor base**: Push sausage to the edges of the pot. Add butter, then leeks, carrots, and celery. Cook, stirring, for 3 minutes until they begin to soften. Stir in garlic, thyme, red pepper flakes, and sage; cook for 2 minutes until fragrant.
5. **Simmer**: Pour in the hot chicken stock, scraping up any browned bits. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for about 10 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
6. **Finish the soup**: Stir in the roasted squash, spinach, parmesan, and cream. Cook until the spinach is wilted and the soup is heated through. Season with salt to taste.
7. **Serve**: Ladle the soup over the cooked pasta in bowls. Top with additional parmesan if desired.
