## Directions

1. **Prepare and Sear the Meat**
2. - Preheat your oven to 300 degrees F.
3. - Generously season the meat with about 1 ? teaspoons of Kosher salt and 1 teaspoon of ground black pepper on all sides.
4. - Heat about 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large 7-8 quart Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
5. - Sear the meat on each side until golden brown, about 5-6 minutes per side.
6. - Remove the meat from the pot and set it aside.
7. - Remember not to wipe the Dutch oven clean, as the flavor from the searing will enhance the dish.
8. **Saute Onions and Garlic**
9. - Reduce the heat to medium and add the onions to the Dutch oven.
10. - Saute them until they start to brown, about 3 minutes.
11. - Scrape the bottom and sides of the pot to deglaze it with the moisture from the onions.
12. - If the brown bits are burning too fast, add a few splashes of broth to help deglaze.
13. - Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
14. - Use a wooden spoon to avoid scratching the cast iron surface.
15. **Create the Sauce**
16. - Add red wine (or substitute with beef broth) to the pot and quickly deglaze the brown bits from the bottom and sides.
17. - Then, add 2 cups of beef broth, Worcestershire sauce, tomato paste, fresh thyme sprigs, fresh rosemary sprigs, and bay leaves.
18. - Stir until everything is evenly combined, and bring the mixture to a low boil.
19. **Slow Cook the Meat**
20. - Return the seared meat back to the pot, along with any juices.
21. - The liquid should cover about half of the meat?add more beef broth if necessary.
22. - Cover the pot securely with the lid and bake in the oven at 300 degrees F for 1.5 hours.
23. **Add Vegetables and Continue Cooking**
24. - Remove the pot from the oven and add baby carrots and potatoes.
25. - At this stage, you can also adjust the seasoning with additional salt and pepper if needed.
26. - Place the lid back on and return the pot to the oven.
27. - Cook for another 2 hours, or until the meat is fork-tender and falls apart, and the vegetables are tender.
28. - For even more tender meat, consider cooking for an additional 15-30 minutes.
29. **Garnish and Serve**
30. - If desired, garnish the dish with fresh parsley for added flavor and color.
31. - To serve, you can transfer the meat and vegetables to a large platter or serve directly from the Dutch oven.
32. - Enjoy your meal!
