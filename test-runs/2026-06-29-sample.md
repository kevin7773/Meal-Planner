## Monday, June 29 - Blackstone Chicken Fajita Brown Rice Bowls

**Theme:** Mexican Monday  
**Estimated Fiber:** 14 grams per serving  
**Active Prep Time:** 25 minutes  
**Cook Time:** 40 minutes  
**Cooking Method:** Blackstone and stovetop

### Ingredients

#### Main Ingredients

- 3 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts, sliced
- 3 bell peppers, sliced
- 2 large yellow onions, sliced
- 2 cups dry brown rice
- 3 1/2 cups water
- 2 (15-ounce) cans pinto beans, drained and rinsed
- 2 cups frozen corn, thawed
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 cup salsa
- 1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt
- 2 limes, cut into wedges
- 1 small seedless watermelon, cut into wedges

#### Seasonings

- 2 teaspoons chili powder
- 2 teaspoons ground cumin
- 2 teaspoons smoked paprika
- 2 teaspoons garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon onion powder
- 1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt
- 1 teaspoon black pepper

### Directions

1. Combine the brown rice, water, and 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, cover, reduce the heat, and simmer for 35-40 minutes. Reserve 3 cups cooked rice for Wednesday.
2. Mix the chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, remaining 1 teaspoon kosher salt, and black pepper.
3. Toss the chicken, peppers, and onions with the olive oil and seasoning mixture. Cook on a medium-high Blackstone, turning regularly, until the chicken reaches 165 F and the vegetables are tender, 10-12 minutes.
4. Reserve 4 cups of the chicken and vegetable mixture for Wednesday. Warm the pinto beans and corn separately.
5. Divide the remaining rice, chicken and vegetables, beans, and 1 1/2 cups corn among four bowls. Serve with salsa, Greek yogurt, lime wedges, and watermelon.

### Leftover Reuse Notes

- Refrigerate 3 cups rice, 4 cups fajita mixture, and 1/2 cup corn for Wednesday.

## Tuesday, June 30 - Slow-Cooker BBQ Chicken Sliders with Apple Slaw

**Estimated Fiber:** 11 grams per serving  
**Active Prep Time:** 20 minutes  
**Cook Time:** 4-6 hours  
**Cooking Method:** Slow cooker

### Ingredients

#### Main Ingredients

- 3 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts
- 1 1/2 cups barbecue sauce
- 12 whole-wheat slider buns
- 2 (15-ounce) cans vegetarian baked beans
- 1 (14-ounce) bag coleslaw mix
- 1 apple, cored and thinly sliced
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
- 1 tablespoon honey

#### Seasonings

- 2 teaspoons smoked paprika
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon onion powder
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon celery seed

### Directions

1. Put the chicken in the slow cooker. Mix the barbecue sauce, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, kosher salt, and black pepper; pour it over the chicken.
2. Cover and cook on low for 5-6 hours or high for 3-4 hours, until the chicken reaches 165 F and shreds easily.
3. Shred the chicken in the sauce. Measure and refrigerate 2 cups for Thursday.
4. Whisk the olive oil, apple cider vinegar, honey, and celery seed. Toss with the coleslaw mix and apple.
5. Warm the baked beans. Fill 8 slider buns with the remaining chicken and serve two sliders per person with slaw and beans. Reserve the other four buns for lunches or freeze them.

### Leftover Reuse Notes

- Refrigerate 2 cups BBQ chicken for Thursday's wraps.

## Wednesday, July 1 - Blackstone Fajita Chicken Fried Brown Rice

**Estimated Fiber:** 9 grams per serving  
**Active Prep Time:** 15 minutes  
**Cook Time:** 12 minutes  
**Cooking Method:** Blackstone

### Ingredients

#### Main Ingredients

- 3 cups reserved cooked brown rice
- 4 cups reserved fajita chicken and vegetables
- 1/2 cup reserved corn
- 1 cup frozen peas
- 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
- 3 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
- 2 teaspoons toasted sesame oil
- 1 lime, cut into wedges

#### Seasonings

- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

### Directions

1. Whisk the soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic powder, ground ginger, and black pepper.
2. Heat the Blackstone to medium-high. Add the fajita mixture, rice, corn, and peas and cook for 5 minutes, tossing until hot.
3. Move the mixture aside. Scramble the eggs on the cleared surface until fully set, then combine them with the rice.
4. Pour the seasoned soy mixture over the rice and cook for 2-3 minutes. Serve with lime wedges.

### Leftover Reuse Notes

- This dinner uses all food intentionally reserved on Monday.

## Friday, July 3 - Grilled Salmon with Chickpea Quinoa Salad

**Estimated Fiber:** 11 grams per serving  
**Active Prep Time:** 25 minutes  
**Cook Time:** 20 minutes  
**Cooking Method:** Grill and stovetop

### Ingredients

#### Main Ingredients

- 1 1/2 pounds salmon, cut into 4 fillets
- 1 cup dry quinoa, rinsed
- 2 cups water
- 1 (15-ounce) can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 1 pound green beans, trimmed
- 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1 cucumber, diced
- 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley
- 1 lemon
- 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
- 1 teaspoon honey

#### Seasonings

- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon dried dill
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt, divided
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper, divided

### Directions

1. Combine the quinoa, water, and 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt. Bring to a boil, cover, reduce the heat, and simmer for 15 minutes. Rest covered for 5 minutes.
2. Toss the salmon and green beans with 1 tablespoon olive oil, garlic powder, dried dill, 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, and 1/4 teaspoon black pepper.
3. Grill the salmon skin-side down over medium heat for 4-6 minutes per side, until it reaches 145 F. Grill the green beans for 6-8 minutes until crisp-tender.
4. Whisk 2 tablespoons olive oil with the juice and zest of the lemon, honey, remaining 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt, and remaining 1/4 teaspoon black pepper.
5. Toss the quinoa, chickpeas, tomatoes, cucumber, parsley, and lemon dressing. Serve with the salmon and green beans.

### Leftover Reuse Notes

- Extra quinoa salad can be used for Saturday lunch.

## Saturday, July 4 - Blackstone Turkey Cheesesteak Baked Potatoes

**Estimated Fiber:** 10 grams per serving  
**Active Prep Time:** 20 minutes  
**Cook Time:** 18 minutes  
**Cooking Method:** Microwave and Blackstone

### Ingredients

#### Main Ingredients

- 1 1/2 pounds 93%-lean ground turkey
- 4 large russet potatoes, scrubbed
- 2 bell peppers, sliced
- 1 large yellow onion, sliced
- 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 4 slices provolone cheese
- 2 cups mixed berries

#### Seasonings

- 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon onion powder
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

### Directions

1. Pierce the potatoes and microwave them for 12-16 minutes, turning halfway, until easily pierced with a fork.
2. Mix the smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, kosher salt, and black pepper.
3. Heat the Blackstone to medium-high. Cook the turkey in the olive oil with half the seasoning mixture until browned and it reaches 165 F, 7-9 minutes.
4. Add the peppers, onion, mushrooms, and remaining seasoning mixture. Cook for 6-8 minutes until tender, then combine with the turkey.
5. Split the potatoes and fill each with turkey and vegetables. Top each with one provolone slice and cover briefly to melt. Serve with berries.

### Leftover Reuse Notes

- Refrigerate any extra filling for omelets or lunch bowls.

## Sunday, July 5 - Grilled Lemon-Herb Chicken with Chickpea Cucumber Salad

**Estimated Fiber:** 12 grams per serving  
**Active Prep Time:** 25 minutes  
**Cook Time:** 15 minutes  
**Cooking Method:** Grill

### Ingredients

#### Main Ingredients

- 1 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts
- 1 (15-ounce) can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 1 cucumber, diced
- 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1/2 red onion, finely diced
- 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
- 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
- 2 lemons
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 4 whole-wheat pitas
- 1 cup hummus
- 2 cups mixed berries

#### Seasonings

- 1 1/2 teaspoons dried oregano, divided
- 1 1/4 teaspoons kosher salt, divided
- 3/4 teaspoon black pepper, divided

### Directions

1. Whisk 1 tablespoon olive oil, the juice of one lemon, minced garlic, 1 teaspoon dried oregano, 3/4 teaspoon kosher salt, and 1/2 teaspoon black pepper. Coat the chicken and refrigerate for 15 minutes while the grill heats.
2. Grill the chicken over medium-high heat for 5-7 minutes per side until it reaches 165 F. Rest for 5 minutes before slicing.
3. Whisk the remaining 2 tablespoons olive oil with the juice of the second lemon, remaining 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano, remaining 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, and remaining 1/4 teaspoon black pepper.
4. Toss the chickpeas, cucumber, tomatoes, red onion, parsley, and dressing.
5. Serve the chicken with chickpea salad, whole-wheat pitas, hummus, and berries.

### Leftover Reuse Notes

- Leftover chicken and salad can become Monday lunch pitas.
